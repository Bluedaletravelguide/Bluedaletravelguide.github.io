KL Upside Down House

KL Tower Upside Down House is one of the newest attractions in KL Tower. It was designed with a 'Cottage House' theme, with an antique 'Morris 1974' hanging upside down at the entrance. It offers a new experience where not only the house but all the furniture in the house is upside down.

Read More

Kuala Lumpur Upside Down House at KL Tower is the tallest of its kind in the capital of Malaysia, featuring a traditional two-storey English cottage, complete with furniture and home décor that, is completely upside down. Located outside the famous tower, it is also within walking distance of the family-friendly attractions of Jalan P. Ramlee. The main entrance provides interesting photo opportunities as there is a neon yellow (and upside down) vintage car above the guests. Kuala Lumpur Upside Down House is also surrounded by the upside-down garden, with vibrant artificial flowers, grass and trees, as well as gazebos overlooking the KL City Centre. This place is sure to knock your child’s socks off.

KL Tower Mini Zoo

If you are looking for kid-friendly places in KL, KL Tower Mini Zoo should be on your list. A wide variety of exotic animals can be found here, ranging from large python monkeys to cuddly rabbits. Take a good shot of the stunning and colourful parrots.

Read More

Sitting at the bottom of the KL Tower-one of the attractions of Kuala Lumpur City, KL Tower Mini Zoo (KLTMZ) is the newest petting zoo in Malaysia. This strategic position provides convenience for visitors, particularly foreign ones, to access public transport, including the LRT, the monorail and the free tour bus. KLTMZ is carefully planned and specially built as a destination of choice for departing families as well as for those who would like to be near to more than 50 species of animals, ranging from native species to exotic animals.

Deer Park

Wildlife enthusiasts should take a chance to enter the majestic Deer Park to witness tons of the adorable namesake animal roaming around the park. Housed within a two hectare enclosure, the locally beloved Deer Park is home to the medium-sized fallow deer (also known as Dama Dama) from Holland with a pretty spotted coat. This park is also situated to other popular attractions such as the Tun Abdul Razak Memorial and the Orchid Garden.

Bamboo Playhouse

The Bamboo Playhouse is a public pavilion in the Perdana Botanical Gardens, Kuala Lumpur's oldest and possibly most beautiful park. The pavilion is located on a small island in the middle of a big lake that runs through the park. Bamboo is rarely used in modern buildings in Malaysia, and this structure investigates its possibilities as a sustainable building material.
